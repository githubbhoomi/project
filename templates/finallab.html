Computer Lab

A computer science lab is a dedicated space equipped with computers and other technological resources for conducting experiments, research, and practical exercises related to the field of computer science. In a computer science lab, students and researchers can work on coding, software development, data analysis, artificial intelligence, networking, and various other areas of computer science. They can run simulations, test algorithms, develop software applications, and explore the theoretical and practical aspects of computing.




Physics Lab

A physics lab is a dedicated space equipped with scientific instruments and equipment for conducting experiments and practical demonstrations in the field of physics. In a physics lab, students can study and explore various laws and principles of physics through hands-on experiments. They can conduct experiments related to mechanics, optics, electricity, magnetism, thermodynamics, and other branches of physics. They can measure physical quantities, verify theoretical predictions, analyze data, and gain practical insights into the fundamental concepts of the discipline.






Chemistry Lab

A chemistry lab is a specialized facility designed for conducting experiments and investigations in the field of chemistry. It is equipped with a wide range of apparatus, chemicals, and instruments necessary for chemical analysis and synthesis. In a chemistry lab, students and researchers can perform experiments to study chemical reactions, identify substances, analyze samples, measure properties such as pH and temperature, and explore various concepts and principles of chemistry. They can also learn about safety protocols and good laboratory practices.
